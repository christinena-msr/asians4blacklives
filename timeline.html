1955

Emmett Till murdered

Emmett Till, 14, was accused of flirting with a white woman at a store he visited in Money, Mississippi. After hearing of the incident, the woman's husband and his half-brother lynched Till and threw him in the Tallahatchie River. His body was found three days later. The men were found not guilty of the murder at trial, but openly confessed one year later.

1957

Little Rock Nine

Nine Black children enrolled at the all-white Central High School in Little Rock, Arkansas. On their first day of classes, the governor ordered the National Guard to block their entry. The National Guard was removed by federal order, and the police escorted the nine students inside. Their names were Minnijean Brown, Elizabeth Eckford, Ernest Green, Thelma Mothershed, Melba Patillo, Gloria Ray, Terrence Roberts, Jefferson Thomas and Carlotta Walls.

1960

Ruby Bridges attends William Frantz Elementary School

Ruby Bridges becomes the first Black child to attend the all-white school in New Orleans, Louisiana. She was one of six Black children who passed an entrance exam to allow them to attend the school. Two of the children decided to stay at their own school, while the other three went to McDonogh No. 19. They were Leona Tate, Tessie Prevost, and Gail Etienne, nicknamed the McDonogh Three.

1989

Five boys are sentenced to life in prison after being convicted of the rape and assault of a woman in Central Park. None of these boys had any conection to the crime.

1992

LA Riots

Rodney King was brutally beat by police officers during his DUI arrest. The jury acquitted 4 officers from "usage of excess force". These officers faced no punishment for their actions. Riots erupted in mostly Koreatown partially due to the racial tensions between Koreans and Black people.

1994

Violent Crime Control and Law Enforcement Act

This new bill provided for 100,000 more police officers to "fight" crime. $9.7 billion in prison funding, and $6.1 billion in prevention programs.


Three Stikes Law

This new law mandated life sentence in prison for 3 convictions. Many states have enacted similar laws at the state level.

2008

Barack Obama becomes the first Black man to be elected President of the United States of America.
